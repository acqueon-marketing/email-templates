Countdown to CMS Medicaid & CHIP Continuous Enrollment Unwinding Mandate: What You Need to Know

Have you been following the impending CMS mandate? The Center for Medicare & Medicaid Services recently announced a Medicaid & CHIP (Children’s Health Insurance Program) “Unwinding” program—that’s a lot of words to say one thing: Everyone covered by Medicaid or CHIP must immediately certify their eligibility to stay covered. By everyone, we mean 87 million individuals in the United States, a number and eligibility/enrollment effort that’s created a looming risk for yet another public health crisis.

There’s a ton to unpack if you have Healthcare Providers or State customers, but here are the Cliffs Notes:

  1. If State-level Health and Human Services departments can’t reach the 87 million people using Medicaid & CHIP, those individuals will lose health coverage.

  2. Healthcare Providers are facing a massive uptick in claim denials due to lost coverage and/or new coverage for any individuals who are deemed no longer eligible.

Do Your Customers Understand the Importance of Preemptive Collections?

For retail banks and financial services firms, preempting consumer account issues and addressing them proactively is one of the most effective strategies to reduce delinquencies.

Here are a few examples of preemptive campaigns your Collections customers can implement using Acqueon:

Collections Campaign Use Case #1: Credit Remaining

If a consumer’s remaining credit dips below a certain amount, send campaigns that educate consumers on the effects this will have on their credit score. Preemptively educating consumers increases customer experience scores, results in less outstanding debt, and creates opportunities for up-selling via a new line of credit, personal loan, or another timely, relevant offer.

Collections Campaign Use Case #2: Credit Score

Similar to third-party apps like Credit Karma, retail banks can provide credit score updates to consumers. Higher credit scores give agents new, warm opportunities for cross-selling and up-selling, while low credit scores provide a timely opportunity to teach consumers how to better manage their credit, debt & overall financial health.

Collections Campaign Use Case #3: New Products

With insight into consumer utilization rates and credit scores in one unified platform, retail banks and financial services firms can more easily sell new products. As new products and feature innovations are released, consumers with low utilization and high credit scores can be added to campaign chains for offers they have a high propensity of accepting.
