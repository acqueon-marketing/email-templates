Contact Center Compliance Risk & How Acqueon Can Support Your Customers

Staying up to date with the latest compliance regulations is hard. It’s especially difficult for contact center executives who already have a lot of responsibility. However, it’s critical to stay current and maintain compliance. The consequences of non-compliance in a contact center can be monumental.

Below are the biggest risks your customers face and how to prevent costly missteps using Acqueon:

Outdated or Inaccurate Data

Depending on how your customers obtain contact information for their lists, they may be acquiring outdated or inaccurate data. As data plays a major role in the larger compliance picture, using bad data or the wrong information is a fast path to costly compliance violations.

Violating DNC Rules

DNC violations can carry penalties as high as $43,792 per call and compliance litigators are highly focused on companies that have violated DNC rules. Additionally, there’s a renewed emphasis on telemarketing regulations at the state level, raising the DNC violation risk level overall. There are also federal and internal lists your customers must comply with, too.

Personal Liability for TCPA Violations

In some cases, it’s possible for executives to be personally targeted for litigation based on TCPA violations. With personal liability factored in, it’s critical to educate your customers on the seriousness of TCPA violations.

Reassigned Phone Numbers

Thousands of phone numbers are reassigned to new users every day. Because consent is based around calling the right party (and not the phone number), calling or messaging a number that was reassigned carries a massive compliance risk.
